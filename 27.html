12.6 Filling of orbitals in an atom
(1)
Aufbau Principle:
In the ground state of the atoms, the orbitals are filled in
order of their increasing energies. In other words, electrons
first occupy the lowest energy orbital available to them and
enter into higher energy orbitals only when the lower energy
orbitals are filled.
Unlike H-atom where energy of orbitals depend only on n,
energy of orbitals of multi-electron orbitals depend on both
n and l. Their order of energy can be determined using
(n+l)
rule.
According to this rule, lower the value of (n+l) for an orbital,
lower is its energy. If two different types of orbitals have
the same value of (n+l), the orbital with lower value of n has
lower energy.
